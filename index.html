Tristan Tzara/Dadaist Web Poem:

I'm imagining a keyboard, like the one I'm using to type, coded as a CSS element displayed on the screen. Rather than the usual letters, each key is a word (sourced from the contents of a book, an article, news feed available online). When you click on the word, the word is displayed on the screen above the keyboard, spoken aloud by the computer, and the word of the key you just pressed is replaced by another word. As you press along, the words you've clicked are saved and displayed as a passage.

Bottom Trawling Youtube:

The iceberg is a popular metaphor on youtube for describing the vast amounts of content available to us. Its spurred a number of channels to do "deep dives" into their content of choice. Whether it be a popular game franchise, a cult film, a conspiracy theory, etc -- chances are someone's made an iceberg out of your favorite passtime. My interest in this metaphor is in the way it gives form to the largely dispersed, ephemeral bodies of content that we access on the internet. In its most basic sense, the iceberg metaphor presents us with a peak -- that is, content that has a certain surplus relevance, something we might be familiar with but not know all that well.
